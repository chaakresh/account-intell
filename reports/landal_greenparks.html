🎯 Strategic Direction
Landal, having merged with Roompot and rebranded in 2025, is now a leading European provider of holidays in nature. The company operates over 22,500 accommodations across more than 200 holiday locations in eight countries.
1. CORPORATE VISION & DIRECTION
Leadership is focused on further international growth and a commitment to environmental sustainability, driven by the purpose "We give everyone the freedom to find their happy place". The overarching ambition is to become the "undisputed European market leader in holidays in nature".
Martin Jermiin was appointed as the new Chief Executive Officer (CEO), effective April 7, 2026, succeeding Mikael Andersson, who successfully oversaw the integration of Roompot and Landal GreenParks. Dirk Anbeek, Chairman of the Supervisory Board, noted that Jermiin "combines a focus on sustainable growth with a powerful brand experience, strategic vision and a solid operational foundation. He is a collaborative leader who will stay close to the parks, the teams, homeowners and the guests".
The company's new international headquarters was established in Amsterdam by 2025 to professionalize the organization, foster cooperation, and strengthen connections among colleagues across various locations. Landal will continue to operate from multiple offices in the Netherlands and through regional offices abroad, leveraging an international talent pool.
2. BIG STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
Expansion: Landal is actively expanding its portfolio with new park openings and developments. For 2026, several new Landal parks are available for booking or are in development, including Landal Soof Heuvelrug, Landal Waterpark Oudehaske, Landal Soof aan de Rijn, Landal Olde Kottink, Landal De Thijmse Berg, Landal Buytenplaets Rhenen, Landal Namur Nature, Landal Buitenheem (opening Spring 2026), Landal Parc des Sources in the Belgian Ardennes, Landal De Strabrechtse Vennen, Landal Nature Parc Saint Hubert, Landal De Wedderplassen, and Landal Landgoed Bourtange.
Sustainability Mandates: Landal maintains an active sustainability policy, with all parks being Green Key certified. The organization aims to "strengthen and accelerate its sustainability targets in areas such as CO2 and waste reduction" by utilizing Landal GreenParks' existing expertise. Previous investments included €39 million for the construction of 74 zero-energy holiday homes in Mont Royal, Germany, and the second phase of De Reeuwijkse Plassen in the Netherlands, recognized as a highly sustainable recreational park.
Restructuring & Integration: The merger of Roompot and Landal GreenParks culminated in a unified "Landal" brand, a single commercial platform, and one operating model, a process successfully led by former CEO Mikael Andersson. The complete rollout of the Landal brand across all parks is anticipated to continue until the end of 2026. The integration also involved harmonizing diverse employment conditions into a new, single package for the Dutch operations.
3. TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY
Landal's technology strategy is centered on digitalization and platform consolidation following the merger. The company is leveraging Roompot's expertise in digitalization and project development to accelerate the enhancement of Landal GreenParks locations. A significant initiative involves the unification of the businesses under "one commercial platform, and one operating model". A new Landal website is planned to offer the complete range of holiday parks.
Regarding vendor relationships and build vs. buy signals, Landal GreenParks has sought solutions that are easily integrated into their API and can support their goal of becoming more technology-driven. An example of this is the adoption of Salto Systems for cloud-based smart access control, indicating a "buy" approach for specialized technological solutions. There is no specific stated public stance on AI adoption or extensive details on cloud migration beyond the general push for digitalization and a unified platform.
