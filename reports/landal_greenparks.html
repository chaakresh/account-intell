🎯 Strategic Direction
Landal, the unified brand resulting from the 2023 merger of Landal GreenParks and Roompot, is focused on cementing its position as a leading European holiday park operator. The company's strategic direction for the next 12-18 months is heavily influenced by this integration and a drive for sustainable growth and an enhanced customer experience.
1. CORPORATE VISION & DIRECTION
Leadership is steering Landal towards becoming the "undisputed European market leader in holidays in nature". This vision is underpinned by the ambition to offer the "most attractive and most diverse selection of holiday parks in Europe". The merger itself was intended to provide a "springboard for further international growth".
A significant leadership change occurred with Mikael Andersson stepping down as CEO in January 2026. Martin Jermiin was appointed as the new CEO, effective April 7, 2026, bringing extensive international experience in retail and business transformation, with a focus on sustainable growth and brand experience. During the interim, Chief Transformation & Strategy Officer Martijn Bouquet assumed day-to-day CEO responsibilities, indicating a continued emphasis on strategic transformation.
The company's core purpose is "We give everyone the freedom to find their happy place". To support this, Landal established a single international headquarters in Amsterdam by March 2025 (announced November 2024) to "further professionalise the organisation and work towards a future centred on cooperation and connection". This move aims to enable more efficient collaboration and stronger connections across its international teams.
2. BIG STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
The immediate strategic initiatives are centered around the post-merger integration and expansion:
Integration and Harmonization: A primary focus has been the "successful integration of Roompot and Landal GreenParks into one unified organisation". This includes aligning the operating model, commercial platform, and harmonizing employment conditions across the two previously distinct entities.
Brand Rebranding: The combined organization now operates under the single "Landal" brand, with the rebranding taking effect in 2025. Parks are gradually adopting a new appearance and brand identity.
Expansion: Landal continues its growth trajectory by adding new holiday parks. Recent and planned expansions include new locations in the Netherlands (e.g., Landal Sallandse Heuvelrug, Landal De Brabantse Wal, Landal Soof Heuvelrug, Landal Waterpark Oudehaske) and Great Britain (e.g., Landal Rockingham Forest). Dirk Anbeek, General Manager of Landal GreenParks, noted, "In the Netherlands, we still see sufficient room for growth in this sector. Still, also internationally there are development opportunities for a strong brand such as Landal GreenParks".
Sustainability Mandates: Landal is committed to strengthening and accelerating its sustainability targets, including CO2 and waste reduction. The company leverages Landal GreenParks' established expertise in sustainability and aims for its operations to be "completely climate-neutral by 2030". All Landal parks are Green Key certified, and for the ninth consecutive year as of March 2026, Landal was named the most sustainable brand in the 'Holiday Parks' category within the Sustainable Brand Index. Initiatives include protecting biodiversity, planting new trees, creating wildflower meadows, and reducing chemical use.
Quality Improvements & Investments: The company emphasizes continuous investment in modernizing and innovating its offerings. This includes renovating accommodations, restaurants, and other facilities.
3. TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY
Landal's technology strategy is geared towards enhancing the digital guest experience and streamlining internal operations post-merger:
Digitalization as a Core Competency: Roompot's "expertise in digitalisation and project development" is being utilized to "develop the current Landal GreenParks locations more quickly".
Platform Consolidation: A key focus is the "integration and harmonisation of Roompot and Landal GreenParks' digital systems". Carla Milovanov, CIO, appointed in May 2024, is specifically tasked with this, bringing experience in integrating numerous brands under a single technology stack. The aim is to achieve "one commercial platform, and one operating model".
Hybrid Hospitality Experience: Landal is actively developing a "seamless hybrid hospitality experience" for its guests, working with partners like iO (formerly Intracto) on UX strategy & design, and app & web development. This includes an online booking management system that aims to reduce stress for guests and allow owners to manage rental homes. The Landal app provides guests with practical and recreational information, enabling digital check-in and activity planning.
Vendor Relationships & Build vs. Buy: The company partners with specialized IT providers. For its UK operations, Landal GreenParks UK has partnered with Elite Dynamics, adopting their "EliteParks park management system" as the preferred software solution. This partnership aims to enable shared availability and integrated bookings, indicating a "buy" strategy for core park management software. Concurrently, the collaboration with iO for digital experience development suggests a "co-create" or "build" approach for differentiating guest-facing digital services.
