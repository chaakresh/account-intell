🎯 Strategic Direction
1
CORPORATE VISION & PRIORITIES
Landal's overarching purpose is "We give everyone the freedom to find their happy place". The company aims to be the undisputed European market leader in nature-based holidays. This vision is supported by four core values: "Feel at home," "Together as one," "Discover your nature," and "Focus on what matters". These values guide both the company's culture and brand, fostering a safe and trusted environment where curiosity is encouraged, and a sustainable future and personal happiness are prioritized.
A major corporate priority has been the successful integration of Roompot and Landal GreenParks into a single unified organization. This transformation, led by former CEO Mikael Andersson, included establishing a single international headquarters in Amsterdam by 2025 and aligning their operating model and commercial platform. The full rebranding to the Unverified new "Landal" identity, visible across over 120 resorts, is slated for completion in 2026 and will expand to over 260 resorts across nine European countries. As of January 2026, Mikael Andersson stepped down as CEO, with Martijn Bouquet, Chief Transformation and Strategy Officer, assuming interim CEO responsibilities to ensure continuity in operations and strategic agenda.
2
MAJOR STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
Post-Merger Integration & Rebranding: The most significant ongoing initiative is the integration of Roompot and Landal GreenParks, a process approved by the Dutch Consumer and Market Authority (ACM) in April 2023. This has resulted in a combined entity offering holidays at 305 destinations in 11 European countries. The establishment of a central, international headquarters in Amsterdam by 2025 is a key part of professionalizing the organization and fostering collaboration. The full rebranding of over 260 resorts to the new Landal brand is expected to be completed in 2026.
Sustainable Investment & Operations: Landal is committed to an active sustainability policy, with all its parks being Green Key certified. The company has allocated €300 million for investment over the coming years, with a portion dedicated to making existing parks more sustainable. A notable example is the construction of 74 zero-energy holiday homes at Mont Royal (Germany) and the second phase of De Reeuwijkse Plassen Outdated in the Netherlands, recognized as the most sustainable recreational park in the country. Landal's ambitious goal is for its operations to become completely climate-neutral by 2030.
Digital Transformation for Enhanced Guest Experience: Landal is actively pursuing digital transformation to improve the guest experience and operational efficiency. A key program is the "Seamless Arrival" feature, developed with Salto Systems, which allows guests to open their bungalows directly with the Landal app, bypassing reception check-in. This utilizes Salto's cloud-based access control solution, "Salto KS," and API integration for digital keys. In the UK, Landal GreenParks UK has partnered with Elite Dynamics to implement their EliteParks park management system as the preferred IT business solution for its customers, aiming for shared availability, dynamic pricing, reservations, and bookings.
