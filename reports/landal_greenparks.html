🎯 Strategic Direction
Landal GreenParks, now operating as Landal following its 2023 merger with Roompot, is pursuing an aggressive corporate strategy focused on European market leadership, significant expansion, and deep commitment to sustainability and technological advancement.
1. CORPORATE VISION & DIRECTION
Landal's leadership is steering the company towards becoming the "undisputed European market leader in holidays in nature." This vision is underpinned by the purpose: "We give everyone the freedom to find their happy place."
In a recent leadership transition, Mikael Andersson stepped down as CEO on January 15, 2026. Martijn Bouquet, the Chief Transformation & Strategy Officer, is currently serving as interim CEO while a search for a permanent successor is underway. Mikael Andersson, reflecting on his tenure, stated, “It has been an honour to lead Landal through such a pivotal phase in its history. I am incredibly proud of what we have achieved together – from integrating two strong brands into one unified business to reinforcing our leadership position in the Dutch market."
Key leadership appointments in 2024 included Mikael Andersson as CEO (until his departure in 2026), Tjalling Smit as Chief Commercial Officer (CCO) as of August 1, 2024, and Carla Milovanov as Chief Information Officer (CIO) as of June 1, 2024. Bastiaan Haks serves as the Chief Financial Officer (CFO).
A significant directional move is the establishment of a central, international headquarters in Amsterdam by March 2025. This move aims to professionalize the organization further and enhance collaboration and connectivity across its operations.
2. BIG STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
Landal is engaged in several strategic initiatives to drive its growth and uphold its brand values:
European Expansion: The company continues its aggressive European expansion, focusing on both new and existing destinations in the Netherlands, Belgium, Germany, and Great Britain. As of late 2024, Landal operates over 300 parks with more than 30,000 accommodations across 11 countries, including the Netherlands, Belgium, Germany, Denmark, the United Kingdom, Austria, Switzerland, the Czech Republic, France, Spain, and Luxembourg.
Recent and upcoming park openings for 2025 include Landal Olde Kottink, Landal Arber, Landal Landgoed Bourtange, Landal Duynvallei, Landal De Strabrechtse Vennen, Landal Klein Oisterwijk, and four new parks in the UK.
Previous investments included €101 million for four new resorts in 2018 (Kaatsheuvel and Volendam in the Netherlands, Bad Boltenhagen in Germany, and Maria-Alm in Austria), along with further investments in its Danish and UK parks.
Sustainability Mandates: Landal has an active and comprehensive sustainability policy, with all its parks holding Green Key certification.
Climate Neutrality by 2030: The overarching goal is to achieve complete climate neutrality by 2030.
"Carbon Countdown" Program: This initiative focuses on reducing CO2 emissions by transitioning from fossil fuels to 100% sustainable European wind energy (achieved since 2025), decreasing gas consumption through smart savings and electric heating, constructing gas- and propane-free buildings, and implementing electric logistics.
"Waste Warrior" Program: Landal aims to reduce waste through conscious purchasing, increased reuse, donation of discarded items, and expanded waste separation efforts.
Biodiversity: Initiatives include establishing wildflower meadows and adding beehives and insect hotels to parks to protect and foster local wildlife.
Sustainable Accommodations: They are converting existing accommodations to utilize heat pumps and solar panels, building energy-neutral houses, and using recycled or recyclable materials for interiors.
Quality Improvements & Guest Experience: Landal has allocated €300 million for investments in new building projects, expansion, and making existing parks more sustainable. A significant portion, €197 million, is dedicated to quality improvements, expansions, and new building projects at existing parks, including the construction of 74 zero-energy holiday homes at Mont Royal in Germany. The company prioritizes a "frictionless guest experience" and aims for a "seamless arrival" process, minimizing waiting times at reception.
3. TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY
Technology is a critical component of Landal's strategy to enhance guest experience and operational efficiency.
Digital Transformation & Platform Consolidation: Landal is undergoing an "award-winning digital transformation" to create a seamless, hybrid hospitality experience for guests, from initial discovery to booking and departure.
A primary focus for Carla Milovanov, CIO, is the "integration and harmonisation of Roompot and Landal GreenParks' digital systems" following the merger, aiming for a unified commercial platform and operating model.
Landal has adopted Maxxton's cloud-based platform for comprehensive management of bookings, check-ins, inventory, and reservations. This system provides essential multi-currency and multi-language capabilities crucial for its international expansion. Landal described Maxxton as "at least two years ahead of its time compared to other software systems."
AI Adoption Stance: Landal is actively exploring and implementing Artificial Intelligence (AI) for practical applications in the hospitality sector.
The company won the DDMA AI Hackathon in 2020 for an "end-to-end solution, which is scalable for broader applications within the organization" and "technologically very well put together."
AI and social media are increasingly recognized as important tools for determining travel destinations.
Landal is testing smart solutions, including autonomous robots from Clevon, to streamline operations and enhance services like package delivery. A pilot project is underway, with an ambitious goal to deploy up to 500 robots across European parks.
Vendor Relationships: Landal collaborates with key technology partners:
Salto Systems: For cloud-based access control solutions that facilitate "seamless arrival" and enable guests to use a digital key, which can be shared via WhatsApp.
Elite Dynamics: For IT business solutions, with their EliteParks system becoming the preferred park management software for Landal GreenParks' UK customers.
iO Digital: A long-standing partner for two decades, working on Landal's digital ecosystem and online booking management systems, emphasizing co-creation in their agile development process.
