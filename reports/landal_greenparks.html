🎯 Strategic Direction
Landal GreenParks' corporate strategy, particularly following its integration with Roompot and subsequent acquisition by the KKR-backed European Camping Group (ECG), is centered on pan-European expansion, deepened sustainability commitments, and a streamlined operational and digital platform.
1. CORPORATE VISION & DIRECTION
Leadership is steering Landal GreenParks towards becoming the "most attractive and most diverse selection of holiday parks in Europe" within the next 12-18 months. The ambition is to be at the "undisputed European vanguard of holidays in nature". This vision is underpinned by a strategy of international growth and consolidating market leadership.
Mikael Andersson, the former CEO of Landal (who stepped down January 15, 2026), played a pivotal role in this transformation, successfully integrating Roompot and Landal GreenParks into a unified organization. Upon his departure, Andersson stated, "Landal is in a very strong position today, with a talented leadership team, clear momentum, and an exciting path ahead. I have full confidence in the organisation and in its future." Dirk Anbeek, Chairman of the Supervisory Board, affirmed that Andersson's "vision and dedication have helped build a strong, unified organisation with significant potential for further growth." The company is currently searching for a new CEO, with Martijn Bouquet, Chief Transformation & Strategy Officer, holding interim responsibilities.
2. BIG STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
Pan-European Expansion and Consolidation: The merger of Roompot and Landal GreenParks created an entity with approximately 300 parks and 32,000 accommodations across 11 countries, establishing a "very broad selection in Europe". The transaction aimed to provide "a springboard for further international growth". This expansion strategy continues under the European Camping Group (ECG), which actively acquires new holiday parks across Europe, including recent additions in France and Spain, with the goal of offering "unrivalled choice and exceptional experiences to guests across Europe". These new acquisitions undergo comprehensive modernization to meet high-quality standards.
Sustainability Mandates: Landal GreenParks maintains a "proactive approach to sustainability," with all its resorts achieving Green Key certification. The combined Roompot and Landal organization committed to leveraging Landal's expertise to "further strengthen and accelerate its sustainability targets in areas such as CO2 and waste reduction". ECG emphasizes a "strong dedication to fostering sustainable practices and fulfilling our customers' increasing demand for environmentally responsible options," aiming to be "agents of change, promoting sustainable practices and reducing carbon emissions across all our activities". In line with this, more than 30 campsites are slated to apply for the Green Key label for the first time in 2025. KKR, as the overarching investor, views sustainability as a "key lever to how we create and protect value across KKR, which helps guide where we invest, how we support our portfolio, and how we operate as a firm". KKR is actively investing in solutions for the transition to a low-carbon economy through its Global Climate Strategy.
Operational Restructuring and Digitalization: The integration of Roompot and Landal GreenParks involved a significant transformation, unifying the businesses under "one unified organisation," establishing a "single international headquarters in Amsterdam," and aligning "one commercial platform, and one operating model". This integration also involved implementing a new organizational structure and remuneration policy within nine months. Roompot's "expertise in digitalisation and project development" is being utilized to "develop the current Landal GreenParks locations more quickly".
