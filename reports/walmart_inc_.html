🎯 Strategic Direction
1. CORPORATE VISION & DIRECTION
Walmart's leadership is steering the company towards sustained growth and increased shareholder value over the next 12-18 months by prioritizing a compelling value proposition, speed, and omnichannel capabilities. The company reiterated its full-year guidance for constant currency sales growth between 3.5% and 4.5%, and operating income growth of 6% to 8% for fiscal year 2027.
CEO Doug McMillon has emphasized the profound impact of AI, stating, "It's very clear that AI is going to change literally every job" and that "Walmart would eliminate some jobs over the next three years and create others, but that overall, AI would have a profound impact on Inferred every occupation." Despite these changes, McMillon stresses that people remain central to Walmart's business, encouraging associates to be "lifelong learners" and equipping them with new tools to improve their jobs.
Key aspects of the future direction include:
Customer Value and Convenience: Continuing to resonate with customers through "low prices with convenience" and faster delivery speeds.
Omnichannel Expansion: Scaling tech-powered businesses like advertising and fulfillment services, and enhancing e-commerce capabilities to meet evolving consumer demands.
Digital Transformation: Integrating advanced technologies like AI and data analytics deeper into core operations to redefine efficiency and customer experience.
Profitability Improvement: Reshaping its profit mix to allow for investments in lower prices, associate wages, and experience-enhancing technologies while aiming to grow profit faster than sales.
2. BIG STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
Walmart has several significant strategic initiatives underway, spanning physical expansion, operational efficiency, and sustainability.
Store Expansion and Modernization:
Walmart plans to build or convert over 150 new stores and remodel 650 existing stores across 47 U.S. states and Puerto Rico in the next five years.
Approximately 20 new stores are slated to open in 2026 and early 2027.
These new and remodeled stores will feature "Store of the Future" concepts, including improved layouts, wider aisles, expanded product selections, new digital touchpoints, and enhanced pickup and delivery capabilities.
Healthcare Expansion: The company is significantly expanding its healthcare services, aiming for over 75 Walmart Health centers by early 2025.
Supply Chain Optimization:
Walmart is scaling its drone delivery program, aiming for 100 drone-enabled stores across five states by the end of 2025, with a goal of 95% same-day coverage.
The company operates high-tech consolidation centers, such as those in Colton, California; Minooka, Illinois; and Lebanon, Pennsylvania, which utilize automated technology to handle three times more volume and improve efficiency.
A prepaid consolidation program for suppliers is being expanded to simplify shipping and optimize inventory allocation across its distribution network.
Sustainability Mandates: New and remodeled stores incorporate sustainable design principles, including energy-efficient equipment and lighting, and lower-impact refrigerants. Walmart is also developing a coast-to-coast network of electric vehicle fast-charging stations.
Cost Transformation and Restructuring:
Walmart has undergone organizational restructuring, including the elimination of several hundred corporate support roles tied to store operations and employee training (e.g., market coordinators and some Walmart Academy positions) in 2025. These changes are part of a strategic effort to "simplify operations and better support stores."
In May 2025, Walmart eliminated approximately 1,500 corporate positions in global tech, e-commerce fulfillment, and advertising as part of efforts to reduce layers of management and organizational complexity.
These restructuring efforts are partly driven by a platform consolidation completed over the past year, merging distinct technology operations for Walmart U.S., Sam's Club, and international businesses into one unified global platform.
"Grow with US" Program: Walmart has committed to investing $350 billion in USA-manufactured products by 2030, collaborating with small to medium-sized enterprises.
3. TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY
Walmart's technology strategy is heavily centered on AI adoption, a hybrid cloud approach, and platform consolidation, with a balanced "build vs. buy" philosophy.
AI Adoption Stance:
Walmart is comprehensively integrating AI across its business, from inventory management to customer experience.
The company's approach to AI, particularly "agentic AI," is "surgical," deploying agents for highly specific tasks that can be stitched together to solve complex workflows.
Walmart is developing purpose-built agentic AI tools, leveraging its own data and large language models (LLMs) to drive business impact.
CEO John Furner noted that AI is helping Walmart "serve needs previous technologies could not meet," leading to more personalized shopping and expanded customer interactions.
Specific AI Initiatives and Programs:
Inventory and Supply Chain: AI systems predict demand, optimize stock levels, reduce waste, and enhance supply chain efficiency, with "Route Optimization" now offered as a Software as a Service (SaaS).
In-Store Technology: "Scan & Go" technology uses computer vision to allow customers to skip checkout lines.
Associate Tools: The "Ask Sam" app is an AI-driven assistant that helps associates with queries, and a GenAI tool called "My Assistant" is being rolled out to 2.1 million associates. Walmart also promotes AI literacy among its workforce and uses ChatGPT Enterprise for teams.
Customer Experience: Walmart's GenAI-powered shopping assistant, "Sparky," uses multi-agent orchestration and evolving voice/camera capabilities, with weekly active users increasing over 100%. Customer-facing shopping agents and computer-vision checkout systems are part of the 2024-2025 roadmap.
Partnerships: Walmart partnered with OpenAI in October 2025 to create "AI-first shopping experiences," allowing customers to shop through ChatGPT using Instant Checkout.
Efficiency: LLMs are being used to reduce fashion lead times by an estimated 18 weeks.
Cloud Migration:
Walmart adopted a hybrid cloud approach in 2017, using a combination of on-premises and cloud resources.
Cloud resources are utilized for workloads requiring scalability and flexibility (e.g., e-commerce, supply chain management), while on-premises resources handle low-latency or high-security workloads (e.g., point-of-sale systems).
Walmart partners with Microsoft Azure for its cloud transformation, migrating hundreds of critical applications and re-architecting them to leverage cloud-native features. The company also utilizes Google Cloud.
Walmart has built its own OpenStack-based cloud infrastructure across its facilities and stores, creating a multi-cloud architecture that combines its private cloud with public cloud providers to optimize costs and speed development.
Platform Consolidation:
To achieve greater efficiency and scalability, Walmart has consolidated its e-commerce and marketplace operations into a single global platform. This move has led to a reduction of duplicate roles and a streamlining of technology operations.
Vendor Relationships & Build vs. Buy Signals:
Historically, Walmart favored a "build rather than buy" approach for its strategic information systems, believing it provides a competitive advantage.
Currently, Walmart continues to build its core AI and global platform infrastructure internally to maintain control over the customer experience and market responsiveness.
For specialized technologies, Walmart strategically partners with external providers, such as Symbotic for automated picking and delivery systems, while retaining control over the data and AI models that power them.
The company has developed its own "internal AI foundry" and an "Element" platform capable of rapid AI application creation, demonstrating a strong build capability in AI.

FINANCIAL STRESS SIGNALS
Margin Compression & Cost-Cutting: While Walmart generally reports strong financial performance, its operating margin slipped slightly to 4.3% of net sales in Q1 FY27 (ended April 30, 2026) due to operating expenses rising faster than sales. This increase reflects higher depreciation from automation and omnichannel investments, business reorganization charges, and increased U.S. healthcare costs. However, the operating margin for Q1 2026 was 4.61%, an increase from the prior quarter's 3.73%. The latest twelve months' gross profit margin is 24.9% and the operating profit margin is 4.18%. To combat costs and improve efficiency, Walmart is rolling out a "Prepaid Consolidation" supply chain strategy aimed at simplifying inbound supplier logistics and lowering overall costs.
Layoffs & Restructuring: In May 2026, Walmart announced plans to lay off or relocate Unverified approximately 1,000 corporate employees as part of a restructuring effort to enhance the efficiency of its global technology and product teams. These changes target overlapping roles across global technology, AI product work, e-commerce, and advertising, following a similar restructuring in the previous year that impacted around 1,500 positions.

🔍 Competitive & Vendors
1. Current Tech Vendors
Walmart operates with a sophisticated and evolving technology stack, heavily leveraging cloud infrastructure, artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning (ML), and in-house developed platforms.
Cloud Platforms: Walmart has adopted a multi-cloud strategy, primarily utilizing Microsoft Azure as its chosen cloud platform. They also deploy workloads on Google Cloud and maintain a significant private cloud infrastructure. The "Walmart Cloud Native Platform (WCNP)" is a Kubernetes-based platform that runs containerized workloads across these edge (stores, distribution centers) and public cloud environments.
AI/Machine Learning Platforms: Walmart has developed an in-house AI/ML platform named "Element." This platform is built on Kubernetes and is designed to run across Walmart's private cloud, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Azure. Element helps data scientists build and scale AI/ML solutions efficiently, offering access to various tools and standardizing processes. They are also developing proprietary Generative AI (GenAI) platforms, including "Wallaby," a series of retail-specific Large Language Models (LLMs) trained with Walmart's data for customer-facing experiences. Additionally, they utilize an AI-powered Customer Support Assistant and an Augmented Reality (AR) platform called "Retina".
Data Platforms: Walmart's data transformation and AI experiences are powered by a "triplet model" multi-hybrid cloud. The company utilizes multiple Business Intelligence (BI) and analytic tools, including a Teradata warehouse, NoSQL databases, SAS, and Hadoop technologies. "Retail Link" is a foundational platform providing suppliers with direct access to Walmart's data grid for real-time inventory tracking and insights.
General Technology Stack: The broader tech stack includes:
Application & Data: JavaScript, Python, AngularJS, Java, React, Spring Boot, Node.js, MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, DynamoDB, Firebase.
DevOps: GitHub, Ansible, Docker, Kubernetes.
Business Tools: Slack, Jira, Microsoft Office 365.
Operating Systems: Linux, Windows, macOS.
Web Servers: Apache, Nginx.
Storage Solutions: AWS S3, Google Firebase Storage.
ERP, CRM, PLM, HCM: While specific traditional ERP, CRM, PLM, and HCM vendors are not explicitly detailed in recent public information, Walmart has partnered with Salesforce to make its Walmart Commerce Technologies and Walmart GoLocal applications available through Salesforce AppExchange. This suggests Salesforce is leveraged for commerce-related CRM or order management functionalities. The significant investment in internal development and cloud-native solutions implies a customized or componentized approach for these enterprise functions, potentially reducing reliance on single, off-the-shelf monolithic systems for core retail operations.
