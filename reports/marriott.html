🎯 Strategic Direction
Marriott's corporate strategy for the next 12-18 months is heavily focused on global expansion, technological transformation with a strong emphasis on AI, and enhancing guest experience across its diverse brand portfolio.
1
CORPORATE VISION & DIRECTION
Marriott's leadership, under President and CEO Anthony Capuano, is steering the company towards "Enterprise Transformation," balancing growth with strategic investment and an evolving consumer landscape. Capuano emphasizes that "technology modernization is becoming foundational to Marriott's long-term growth strategy". The company aims to accelerate its net unit growth, leveraging its global footprint and a proactive conversions strategy, particularly as new construction faces challenges.
Key aspects of their vision include:
Continued Global Expansion: Marriott aims for a net rooms growth between 4.5% to 5% in 2026, building on a record pipeline of 610,000 rooms at the end of 2025. This includes significant growth in the luxury segment with nearly 35 new luxury hotel openings planned for 2026 across brands like St. Regis, Ritz-Carlton, W Hotels, and JW Marriott. Expansion is also notable in midscale offerings, such as the City Express by Marriott brand, particularly in regions like the Caribbean and Latin America (CALA) and EMEA.
Elevating the Loyalty Program: Marriott Bonvoy remains a strategic asset, with efforts to enhance its value for members through credit card offerings, loyalty partnerships, and unique experiences. The program grew to nearly 271 million members at year-end 2025, adding 43 million new members.
Addressing Evolving Traveler Expectations: Marriott is focusing on experiences that balance personal well-being with connection and discovery, particularly within its luxury portfolio. Tina Edmundson, President of Luxury at Marriott International, noted, "Luxury has evolved from being about the things you have to how you feel". This includes culturally enriching experiences, wellness programs, and offerings designed for multi-generational and group travel.
Financial Performance: For 2026, Marriott expects global RevPAR growth of 1.5% to 2.5%. Adjusted EBITDA is projected to increase between 8% to 10%, reaching roughly $5.8 billion to $5.9 billion, and adjusted diluted EPS growth is expected between 13% to 15%. The company also plans to return over $4.4 billion to shareholders in 2026 through dividends and buybacks.
2
BIG STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
Marriott's strategic initiatives are largely intertwined with its digital and technology transformation, aiming for efficiency, enhanced guest experience, and sustainable growth.
Technology Transformation & Replatforming (Project Catalyst): Marriott has committed over $1.1 billion in investment spending in 2026, with more than one-third dedicated to digital and technology transformation. The core of this initiative is "Project Catalyst," a multi-year effort to replatform its three most critical technology platforms: the property management system, central reservations infrastructure, and loyalty platform. These systems have moved from development into deployment, with rollouts planned for a "meaningful number" of hotels in 2026. This transformation aims to move away from fragmented legacy systems to cloud-native environments, enabling unified guest data and real-time analytics.
Luxury Expansion: Marriott is focused on strengthening its global presence in the luxury segment with nearly 35 new luxury hotel openings planned in 2026. This expansion emphasizes immersive, wellness-driven, and culturally enriching experiences in high-demand destinations such as Japan, India, and the UAE.
Midscale Segment Growth: The company has seen "extraordinary growth" in the midscale segment since entering it in 2023, with a strategic focus on regionally resonant brands like City Express by Marriott and Four Points Express by Sheraton.
Sustainability Mandates (Serve 360): Marriott's "Serve 360" platform guides its sustainability and social impact goals for 2026-2030. Key environmental targets include reducing carbon intensity by 30%, water intensity by 15%, and waste to landfill by 45% (with a 50% cut in food waste) by 2025. It also aims for 30% renewable electricity across its portfolio by 2025 and has set an ambitious target of achieving net-zero emissions across its entire value chain by 2050. Marriott will also set a single-use plastic reduction goal by the end of 2025 and disclose a baseline footprint of single-use plastics starting in 2026. Social impact goals include contributing 15 million hours of volunteer service globally and completing 7.5 million training courses on welcoming all.
3
TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY
Marriott's technology strategy is centered on building an AI-ready ecosystem through cloud-native platforms, unified data, and strategic vendor relationships.
AI Adoption Stance: Marriott is "actively investing" in AI, shifting from experimentation and piloting in 2025 to scaling AI initiatives in 2026. CIO Naveen Manga states that 2026 is "a year for scale" for AI. CEO Anthony Capuano views AI as a "long-term opportunity to redefine how hotels acquire, serve, and retain guests". The company is taking a "disciplined approach" to unlock value for guests, associates, and owners.
AI Use Cases: Marriott is focusing on AI to improve guest experience through natural language search on its website and app, powered by OpenAI, and exploring agentic booking integrations with Google's AI Mode. They are also evaluating tools to assist front desk associates and building platforms for owners to gain streamlined insights into hotel operations. Operational AI tools are being used to improve labor scheduling and supply chain logistics to manage inflationary pressures.
"Agentic Mesh": Marriott is developing an "agentic mesh," a shared intelligence layer that will allow AI capabilities to be reused across operations, marketing, customer service, and revenue optimization.
Data as a Foundation: Drew Pinto, Executive Vice President and Chief Revenue and Technology Officer, emphasizes that "problems that we think are technology problems are actually data problems". This drives a renewed focus on data infrastructure and governance to ensure clean, unified, and accessible data for AI models.
Cloud Migration & Platform Consolidation: Marriott is undergoing a multi-year digital transformation to rearchitect its entire technology backbone around AI. This involves migrating core platforms—property management, central reservations, and loyalty systems—to cloud-native environments. The primary drivers for this migration are to improve guest experience, enable agility and innovation, and move away from data center constraints. The company has invested heavily, with $1.8 billion in capital and technology expenditures since 2023, and another $1.1 billion planned for 2026, with about 35% allocated to replatforming efforts.
Vendor Relationships: Marriott is collaborating with technology leaders like OpenAI and Google for AI-powered features. The company has also selected Agilysys as a property management solution provider for its U.S. and Canada luxury, premium, and select-service properties. Furthermore, Marriott signed a Master Service Agreement with Shiji in 2023 to expand the deployment of Infrasys Cloud POS across multiple regions and selected SevenRooms as its preferred restaurant technology partner. In 2023, Marriott also went live with CXone to consolidate its customer service tech stack across 20 international contact centers.
Build vs. Buy Signals: Marriott's approach suggests a "build for differentiation, buy for commodity" philosophy. They are building internal "agentic mesh" and AI chassis to power various use cases, while also partnering with external vendors for specialized solutions and core infrastructure needs like cloud platforms (e.g., AWS). Drew Pinto stated that the technology Marriott has built will "unlock the full potential of AI".

Marriott: Technology Landscape and Competitive Dynamics
Marriott International is undertaking a significant technological transformation, investing heavily in cloud-based systems, artificial intelligence (AI), and data platforms to enhance customer experience and operational efficiency. The company is a dominant player in the global hospitality market, competing primarily with Hilton, IHG, and Hyatt.
1. Current Technology Vendors
Marriott leverages a diverse technology stack to manage its extensive global operations:
ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning): Marriott has utilized Oracle PeopleSoft ERP for its financial system since 2015, with Accenture involved in its implementation.
CRM (Customer Relationship Management): The company employs a comprehensive CRM strategy, prominently featuring Salesforce CRM, including Service Cloud and Sales Cloud. Marriott's "Empower" platform, built on Salesforce, is used by over 100,000 associates across various teams. Other CRM tools mentioned include Oracle Siebel and MICROS OPERA PMS.
HCM (Human Capital Management): Marriott launched Oracle Fusion Cloud Human Capital Management (HCM) in December 2020 to optimize HR processes and employee experiences. This is noted as one of the largest Oracle Cloud HCM deployments. Older systems like Kronos and SAP SuccessFactors are also referenced in relation to HR and payroll operations.
Cloud & Data Platforms:
Marriott utilizes a multi-cloud architecture with Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI) and Microsoft Azure.
Its data platform is hosted on Amazon Web Services (AWS), integrating batch and streaming data into cloud object storage. Marriott is also developing an AWS-backed Travel Assistant.
Snowflake serves as a key data warehouse, simplifying Marriott's data platform and improving performance and cost savings. Data consumption is often done through Tableau.
For its core IT systems and the Marriott Bonvoy platform, Marriott adopted IBM's public cloud computing platform around 2015.
The company is transitioning to modern, scalable, cloud-based systems with partners such as Amadeus and Agilisys.
Marriott implemented NICE CXone for its global customer service operations in March 2023, consolidating its tech stack.
Artificial Intelligence (AI): Marriott is making substantial investments in AI, with planned expenditures of $1 billion to $1.2 billion in 2024. Key initiatives include an AI Incubator, an AI-powered virtual assistant named RenAI, and a generative AI search tool for vacation rentals. The company is piloting Microsoft 365 Copilot and using generative AI for SEO content generation, contact-center coaching, and trial trip-planning tools. Salesforce's AI-powered tools also aid in personalized upsell offers.
2. Known SI/Consulting Partners
Marriott collaborates with several prominent system integrators and consulting partners:
Accenture: A long-standing partner, Accenture has worked with Marriott on various initiatives, including the implementation of its Oracle-based accounting platform and the creation of a global HR hub powered by Oracle Fusion Cloud HCM. Accenture also acquired Marriott Business Services (MBS) F&A unit to provide finance and accounting services under a 10-year contract and has partnered with Marriott on skill development programs in India.
Publicis Sapient: This firm collaborated with Marriott on developing a natural language-based search tool for Homes & Villas by Marriott Bonvoy.
Oracle Customer Success Services: Provides ongoing support and strategic guidance for Marriott's Oracle Cloud HCM implementation.
NiCE: Partnered with Marriott for the successful implementation of the CXone platform for customer service.

4. Competitive Dynamics
Marriott operates in a highly competitive global hospitality market, with key rivals including:
Top Competitors: Hilton Worldwide Holdings, InterContinental Hotels Group (IHG), and Hyatt Hotels Corporation are consistently identified as Marriott's primary competitors. Choice Hotels is also noted as a significant player, particularly in the midscale segment.
Market Position and Share:
Marriott International is the world's largest hotel company, leading in scale with over 9,500 properties and more than 30 brands across 141 countries and territories as of early 2025.
Hilton ranks second with over 8,600 properties, while IHG has over 6,600 properties, dominating the midscale market. Hyatt is smaller, with around 1,350 properties, but strong in luxury and lifestyle segments.
Marriott's Bonvoy loyalty program is the largest in the world, with over 237 million members, and members account for approximately 50% of total sold room nights, providing a significant competitive advantage. Around 70% of Marriott's room nights are booked by loyalty members, positioning it strongly against Online Travel Agencies (OTAs).
Gaining or Losing Market Share:
Marriott is actively focused on growth in luxury and all-inclusive segments, leveraging its extensive global distribution and loyalty program to maintain dominance.
The company has a substantial pipeline of new rooms (620,500 rooms in construction or contract), with a focus on new mid-scale and extended-stay brands, indicating continued expansion.
Marriott's business model, heavily reliant on management contracts, results in a gross margin 20% higher than Hilton's, suggesting greater value extraction per property despite a potentially slower growth rate compared to Hilton's franchise-heavy model.
Marriott's revenue reached $25.10 billion in 2024. While Marriott shows strength in asset efficiency and diversification, it faces challenges with equity returns and liquidity.
The overall hospitality market experienced significant growth, reaching $1.5 trillion worldwide in 2023, suggesting a favorable environment for major players like Marriott and Hilton, whose asset-light models and strong loyalty programs contribute to widening their competitive moats.

1. RECOMMENDED PITCH ANGLE
AI-Enabled Transformation Play
Marriott is in year two of a multi-billion dollar technology replatforming initiative (Project Catalyst) specifically designed to create an AI-ready ecosystem. With $1.1 billion allocated for 2026 (35% to digital transformation), CTO Naveen Manga has explicitly stated that 2026 is "a year for scale" for AI, moving from pilots to production. The company is building an "agentic mesh" shared intelligence layer and deploying cloud-native property management, central reservations, and loyalty systems across a meaningful number of hotels in 2026. This creates immediate urgency for solutions that accelerate AI adoption, ensure clean unified data for AI models (addressing Drew Pinto's stated belief that "technology problems are actually data problems"), or integrate with their new cloud-native architecture during active deployment rather than post-implementation.
2. CONVERSATION OPENER QUESTIONS
You're rolling out Project Catalyst's replatformed property management and loyalty systems to a meaningful number of hotels in 2026. As you move these cloud-native platforms from development into production deployment, what's proving most challenging about ensuring data quality and consistency across properties to support the agentic mesh you're building?
Naveen mentioned 2026 is the year to scale AI beyond pilots. You're exploring agentic booking with Google AI Mode and natural language search with OpenAI, but also building internal AI capabilities for labor scheduling and supply chain optimization. Where are you finding the biggest gap between the AI use cases you want to deploy and the data infrastructure readiness to support them?
With the new regional leadership structure effective March 28th—consolidating US, Canada, and CALA under Satya Anand—and Jen Mason stepping into the CFO role April 1st, how is the technology roadmap being re-prioritized to support this unified regional operating model and the stated $80-90 million G&A cost reduction target?
You've selected specific vendors like Agilysys for PMS and Shiji for POS, but you're building proprietary systems for your agentic mesh and AI chassis. How are you drawing the line between what you build internally for competitive differentiation versus what you integrate from best-of-breed vendors, especially as you scale AI in 2026?
The India expansion achieved 75 hotel signings in six months through Series by Marriott, adding 3,556 rooms across 43 cities. As you target 250 operating hotels and 50,000 rooms in India by 2030, how are you ensuring your replatformed technology systems can scale to support this rapid tier 2 and tier 3 market expansion with the same level of guest experience and operational efficiency?
3. LANDMINES TO AVOID
Do not position as a replacement for their massive ongoing investment in Project Catalyst or suggest their current replatforming approach is wrong. They have committed $1.8 billion since 2023 with another $1.1 billion in 2026 and are in active deployment. Frame as acceleration, augmentation, or de-risking of their existing transformation, not a course correction.
Avoid generic cybersecurity pitches or referencing the past data breaches as a primary pain point without extreme sensitivity. While they settled with the FTC and are enhancing security programs, leading with "we heard about your breach problem" will immediately create defensiveness with new leadership trying to move forward.
Do not ignore or dismiss their existing vendor relationships, especially recently selected partners like Agilysys, Shiji, SevenRooms, NICE CXone, Snowflake, and AWS. They made deliberate choices in 2023-2025. Position around integration with, enhancement of, or complementary capabilities to these platforms rather than replacement.
Avoid speaking about the hospitality industry generically or treating Marriott like a typical hotel chain. They are the world's largest hotel company with 30+ brands, 9,500+ properties across 141 countries, and 237+ million Bonvoy members. They view themselves as a technology and loyalty platform company that happens to operate in hospitality. Speak to their scale, complexity, and platform ambitions.

Frame the objective as: "We'd like to understand your current deployment sequencing for the replatformed property management, central reservations, and loyalty systems across your hotel portfolio in 2026, and share how [your solution] has helped similar enterprise-scale deployments de-risk AI readiness, accelerate data unification, or reduce integration complexity during active cloud migration—with the goal of determining if there's a logical pilot opportunity within your 2026 deployment wave."
