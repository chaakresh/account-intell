🎯 Strategic Direction
Marriott International's corporate strategy for the next 12-18 months (2026-early 2027) is characterized by a "Growing Forward" approach, focusing on global expansion, technological transformation, and deepening loyalty, while maintaining a disciplined financial outlook.
1. CORPORATE VISION & DIRECTION
Under the leadership of President and CEO Anthony Capuano, Marriott is driving an "Enterprise Transformation" that centers on global ecosystem growth, technology modernization, and leveraging artificial intelligence (AI) to enhance efficiency and guest experience. Capuano emphasizes a dual focus: "deepening engagement within the Marriott Bonvoy loyalty program and accelerating net room growth globally." The company's overarching vision is to be "at the center of how people dream, plan, and ultimately experience travel." Marriott aims to balance near-term market volatility, particularly in the U.S. due to consumer confidence, with strong long-term fundamentals for global travel.
For 2026, Marriott projects a global RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room) growth of 2% to 3%. The company expects net rooms growth to be between 4.5% and 5%. Gross fee revenues are anticipated to increase by 9% to 10%, reaching $5.93 billion to $5.99 billion. Adjusted EBITDA is forecasted to grow by 8% to 10%, landing between $5.8 billion and $5.9 billion. Adjusted diluted earnings per share (EPS) growth is projected at 13% to 15%. Marriott plans to return over $4.4 billion to shareholders in 2026 through dividends and buybacks.
Marriott's development pipeline reached a record of nearly 618,000 rooms, with 43% already under construction, indicating robust future growth. The company is seeing strong leisure demand, particularly in the luxury and premium segments, and robust global group travel. International markets continue to be a significant growth driver, with a raised outlook for the U.S., Canada, and China. India is identified as a crucial growth market, targeted to become Marriott's third-largest. The company remains committed to its asset-light business model, focusing on management and franchising rather than real estate ownership.
2. BIG STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
Expansion: Marriott is aggressively expanding its global footprint. It aims for 4.5% to 5% net rooms growth in 2026, with an ambition to surpass 1.8 million open rooms by 2028–2030. Conversions of existing hotels to Marriott brands are a major growth engine, accounting for over 35% of signings and 40% of openings in Q1 2026.
Luxury Segment: Marriott signed a record 114 luxury deals in 2025, with nearly 35 luxury hotels slated to open in 2026 across Europe, Asia, the Middle East, and the Americas. The 2026 luxury roadmap focuses on well-being and longevity, social connection, and culturally rooted transformational experiences.
Midscale & Extended Stay: To penetrate new markets and appeal to a broader traveler base, Marriott is expanding its midscale offerings, including the "Four Points Flex" and "StudioRes" brands. The 2024 acquisition of City Express by Marriott in Latin America is a key part of this strategy, with plans to add thousands of incremental midscale rooms by 2026–2027. By year-end 2025, Marriott's midscale brands accounted for 216 open properties and over 250 in the pipeline.
Geographic Focus: The company is prioritizing growth in high-potential regions such as Greater China, India, the Middle East (driven by initiatives like Saudi Vision 2030), Mediterranean resorts, Mexico/Caribbean, and Southeast Asia.
Sustainability Mandates: Marriott has launched initiatives to embed sustainability across its operations and offerings.
"Connect Responsibly with Marriott Bonvoy Events": This program helps meeting planners integrate sustainability into events by providing Meeting Impact Reports that analyze carbon and water footprints, and offers options to invest in carbon credits.
"Sip for a Cause": Linked to Earth Hour 2026, this campaign raised funds from coffee sales to plant 500 fruit trees and support environmental education in communities.
Marriott's broader sustainability goals, with targets extending beyond 2025 into 2026, include reducing carbon intensity by 30%, water intensity by 15%, and waste to landfill by 45% (with a 50% cut in food waste), and aiming for 30% renewable electricity. The company is committed to having 100% of properties certified to a recognized green building standard.
Cost Transformation/Restructuring: Marriott has undertaken an "enterprise-wide process to enhance effectiveness and efficiency," aiming to achieve $80 million to $90 million in annual pre-tax general and administrative (G&A) cost reductions starting in 2025. These efforts are also designed to deliver cost savings to hotel owners and franchisees. Additionally, a strategic leadership transition and realignment across global operations took effect in March 2026 to streamline operations and foster greater collaboration.
3. TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY
Marriott is committing $1.1 billion in investment spending in 2026, with over one-third dedicated to digital and technology transformation.
Cloud Migration & Platform Consolidation: The company is undergoing a multiyear transformation to retool its core systems and is replatforming three critical systems to cloud-native environments: its property management system (PMS), central reservations infrastructure (CRS), and loyalty platform. Beta launches for these new systems commenced in September 2025, with a phased rollout planned over 18 months. This architectural shift is designed around unified guest data, API-first integrations, and real-time analytics, with a focus on enhancing personalization and fostering faster innovation.
AI Adoption Stance: Marriott is leveraging AI to improve efficiency and guest experiences. CEO Anthony Capuano states, "We see AI as an opportunity to potentially redefine the customer acquisition paradigm that has governed our industry for the past several decades," though he cautions that the technology is still early in its practical application. A key part of the strategy is building an "agentic mesh" or shared intelligence layer, which will support AI agents across various functions like marketing, operations, customer service, and revenue optimization. This aims to create "capacity" for associates, freeing them from manual tasks to deliver a more personal human connection to guests. Marriott's AI initiatives are guided by a four-part framework emphasizing trust, accountability, prioritization, and human-centered innovation.
AI Pilots & Features:
The company is piloting Microsoft Copilot for internal uses like meeting summaries and transcriptions.
AI coaches are being experimented with for call center agents.
An AI-powered trip planning tool is being monitored for Marriott Bonvoy members.
Marriott has launched a "Natural Language Search" feature on its mobile app and Homes & Villas website, powered by OpenAI.
It is also exploring agentic booking integrations with Google's AI Mode.
Generative AI is being used to create content and boost SEO traffic to its website.
Vendor Relationships & Build vs. Buy Signals: Marriott's comprehensive replatforming of core systems indicates a significant "build" component in its technology strategy. However, its partnerships with companies like OpenAI and exploration of integrations with Google's AI Mode suggest a strategic "buy" or partnership approach for specific, advanced AI functionalities. The company also emphasizes building technology "with" its 800,000 associates through end-user councils. Following past data breaches, Marriott is strengthening its cybersecurity, data privacy, and risk management protocols, integrating AI as part of its defense strategy.

Challenges/Intense Competition: The hospitality industry is highly competitive, with rivals constantly vying for market share through competitive pricing, service innovation, brand differentiation, and robust distribution channels. The fragmented technology landscape within the hotel industry, where Property Management Systems (PMS) ownership often rests with property owners and franchisees rather than the brand, can pose challenges for system-wide tech upgrades and AI implementation across its vast portfolio. Marriott's CEO Score ranks 3rd among its competitors, with Hyatt and Four Seasons scoring higher. Market volatility, economic downturns, geopolitical events, and health crises also pose threats to Marriott's growth trajectory. Overall, Marriott is actively working to maintain and grow its market share through strategic investments in technology, brand diversification, and loyalty programs amidst a dynamic competitive environment.
