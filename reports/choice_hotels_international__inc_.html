🎯 Strategic Direction
Choice Hotels International, Inc.'s corporate strategy for the next 12-18 months is heavily focused on driving franchisee return on investment through targeted growth in key segments, significant cost optimization, and extensive adoption of advanced technology, particularly artificial intelligence.
1. CORPORATE VISION & DIRECTION
Choice Hotels' guiding principle for all investments, particularly in technology, is "franchisee return on investment". President and CEO Patrick Pacious stated, "We always start with our 'North Star,' and for us, the 'North Star,' ever since I've been at Choice Hotels… has been franchisee return on investment". This is achieved by increasing top-line revenue, reducing costs, and providing essential business tools to franchisees.
The company aims to accelerate quality room growth across its more revenue-intense brands and markets, which primarily include upscale, extended stay, and midscale segments, while simultaneously increasing effective royalty rates to drive higher royalties. Choice Hotels reported a 4.3% net increase in domestic revenue-intense rooms in 2024 and expects strong long-term growth with 98% of its global pipeline in these brands.
Choice Hotels is also focused on leveraging the revenue synergies unlocked from the integration of Radisson Americas, which contributed to a 17% increase in adjusted EBITDA and 14% increase in adjusted EPS in Q1 2024, achieving record levels for the quarter. The company reaffirmed its full-year 2024 adjusted EBITDA guidance to be in the range of $580 million to $600 million and adjusted EPS between $6.30 and $6.60 per share, anticipating this growth to be driven by incremental contributions from Radisson Americas, including approximately $2 million in additional cost synergies.
International expansion remains a strategic priority, with the company expanding its global footprint through partnerships and acquisitions, including extending its master franchise agreement with Strawberry (formerly Nordic Choice Hotels) and securing a distribution partnership with Spanish hotel chain Sercotel. In 2024, Choice Hotels saw a 4.4% year-over-year increase in its international rooms portfolio and a 58% increase in international hotel openings in Q4 2024.
2. BIG STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
Expansion & Growth: Choice Hotels is aggressively expanding its portfolio, particularly in the upscale, extended stay, and midscale segments.
In 2024, the company opened a record 61 extended-stay hotels, including 25 WoodSpring Suites and six Everhome Suites, bringing its extended-stay property count to over 500. The extended-stay pipeline includes nearly 43,000 rooms.
Upscale growth in 2024 included 27 new hotels, with 18 Ascend Hotel Collection properties and seven Cambria Hotels. The Cambria brand alone has introduced 18 new properties in the last two years and has an expanding pipeline of 62 hotels, with the Cambria New York Times Square expected to open in 2025.
The company emphasizes hotel conversions, with 72% of domestic franchise agreements awarded for conversion hotels in 2023, 135 of which opened in the same year.
Internationally, Choice signed an agreement with Zenitude Hotel-Residences to double its unit footprint in France by adding 33 properties in 2024 and 2025.
A strategic partnership with Westgate Resorts in Q4 2024 added 21 hotels and 14,471 rooms to the domestic portfolio.
Cost Transformation: To enhance owner profitability, Choice Hotels has unveiled "value-engineered prototypes" for its Comfort and Country Inn & Suites by Radisson brands. These prototypes are designed to achieve a 10-15% reduction in construction costs while adding revenue-generating spaces. For example, the Comfort prototype adds three keys within the existing footprint and optimizes costs by 16% through reducing FF&E package SKUs by 30%.
Sustainability Mandates (Commitment to Green Initiative):
Choice Hotels is revamping its "Room to be Green" sustainability certification program, adding a fourth level with elevated environmental standards focusing on renewable energy, gray water management, and smart technologies.
The company is rolling out Schneider Electric's AI-assisted, cloud-based EcoStruxure Resource Advisor, an automated utility tracking dashboard, with a goal to have 100% of its managed and owned hotels using the technology by 2025.
Choice Hotels received the inaugural 2024 World Sustainable Travel & Hospitality Award for installing CarbinX carbon-capture technology at the Radisson Blu Mall of America, making it the first hotel in the world equipped with such technology to reduce CO2 emissions.
The company has expanded its greenhouse gas emissions data to Scope 3 and publicly reported through the CDP Climate and Water surveys for the first time in 2024, in line with its commitment to set science-based targets.
Choice Hotels also announced a partnership with the National Park Foundation in 2024, aligning with its widespread hotel footprint near national parks.
3. TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY
Choice Hotels has positioned itself as a technology leader, with a comprehensive strategy centered on cloud migration and enterprise-wide AI adoption to enhance guest experiences and franchisee profitability.
Cloud Migration: In 2024, Choice Hotels became the "first hotel company to migrate its entire system infrastructure to the cloud" by completely transitioning to Amazon Web Services (AWS). This move concluded a five-year process that involved decommissioning over 3,700 servers and migrating or retiring hundreds of applications. Chief Information Officer Brian Kirkland emphasized the strategic importance, stating, "By moving all of our systems to the cloud, we're ensuring that our franchisees have up-to-the-minute access to the latest innovations, saving time and money while providing industry-leading distribution and digital systems throughout the competitive hotel booking journey". This cloud-first approach provides a flexible, resilient, and scalable platform that enables Choice to leverage AI and respond to real-time market conditions.
AI Adoption Stance: Choice Hotels has embraced AI across its entire enterprise, moving beyond isolated pilot projects to "production-grade deployment at scale". CEO Patrick Pacious asserts, "Across the board, AI isn't just something that sits in the tech department — it's something that's core".
Applications: AI is embedded across the full hospitality value chain, including:
Revenue Management: Tools like ChoiceROCS, ChoiceMAX, and the upcoming RAISE leverage advanced analytics and AI to provide hotel owners with pricing and strategy recommendations, driving stronger Revenue Per Available Room (RevPAR) Index performance. ChoiceMAX, a mobile-first revenue management solution, uses machine learning for real-time pricing.
Guest Experience: AI is used to personalize the end-to-end guest journey, from intelligent search and booking recommendations to on-property experiences, creating more relevant and seamless interactions. The company is investing in a "next-generation, AI-driven guest data platform" to analyze customer data and build unified profiles.
Franchise Operations: AI-powered tools assist hotel owners with smarter decision-making in revenue management, maintenance, and guest communications, reducing friction and improving performance. New solutions like Choice Hotels Business Direct and EasyBid, an AI-enhanced group Request for Proposals (RFP) tool, aim to help owners capture more demand and operate efficiently.
Distribution and Channel Ecosystem: AI reshapes how Choice manages its distribution, enabling smarter pricing, inventory optimization, and partner integrations to drive incremental revenue.
Choice Hotels is actively testing new AI products, including Amazon Connect for customer call centers and generative AI tools like Amazon's CodeWhisperer and Microsoft's Copilot solutions.
Platform Consolidation/Vendor Relationships: Choice Hotels collaborates extensively with major technology providers.
It is the "first to standardize on AgentCore" among major U.S. hospitality providers, an AWS-powered enterprise platform that provides a secure, reusable foundation for intelligent agents capable of retrieving trusted information and automating workflows.
The company also has relationships with Salesforce to utilize AgentForce.
Choice Hotels extended its AI-powered merchandising solution, Oracle Nor1, to its upscale hotels to capitalize on incremental revenue opportunities.
Build vs. Buy Signals: While Choice Hotels partners with leading technology companies like AWS, Salesforce, and Oracle, it also demonstrates a strong "build" signal for core, proprietary systems that provide a competitive advantage. Examples include the internal development of its cloud-based central reservation system, choiceEDGE, in collaboration with AWS, and its mobile-first revenue management solution, ChoiceMAX, developed with SaaS provider IDeaS. This approach allows them to tailor solutions specifically for their franchisees' needs and integrate them deeply into their operations.
