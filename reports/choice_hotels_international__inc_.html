🎯 Strategic Direction
Choice Hotels International, Inc. is prioritizing global expansion, leadership in extended-stay, and advanced technology adoption, particularly artificial intelligence, to drive franchisee profitability and enhance guest experience.
1. CORPORATE VISION & DIRECTION
Leadership aims to drive long-term growth and create meaningful shareholder value over the next 12-18 months by enhancing hotel performance, increasing revenue, reducing operating costs, and providing best-in-class technology tools to franchisees.
Patrick Pacious, former President and CEO, stated in February 2026, "Choice Hotels International delivered another year of record profitability in 2025, driven by our double-digit increase in international rooms, continued leadership in the extended-stay segment, and disciplined portfolio optimization." He also noted, "With a high-quality, accretive global development pipeline, targeted investments that strengthen franchisee economics and customer lifetime value, and a disciplined approach to capital allocation, we believe Choice is exceptionally well positioned to drive long-term growth and create meaningful shareholder value."
For the full year 2025, Choice Hotels reported record profitability, with an adjusted EBITDA of $625.6 million and diluted earnings per share (EPS) of $7.90. The outlook for full-year 2026 anticipates adjusted EBITDA to range between $632 million and $647 million.
As of May 2026, Patrick Pacious stepped down as President and CEO, with Dominic Dragisich, the Chief Growth and Strategy Officer, appointed as interim CEO. The company reaffirmed its full-year 2026 guidance, signaling a commitment to continuity in its strategic direction during this leadership transition.
2. BIG STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
Choice Hotels is advancing several strategic initiatives, focusing on expanding its global footprint, solidifying its extended-stay leadership, strengthening core and upscale brands, and committing to sustainability.
Global Development and Expansion:
In 2025, Choice Hotels achieved record international growth, onboarding 130 new international hotels and expanding its international portfolio by 13% to nearly 160,000 rooms outside the U.S.
The company acquired the remaining 50% stake in Choice Hotels Canada in July 2025, transitioning to a fully direct franchising model to accelerate growth in the Canadian market.
The global pipeline exceeded 77,800 rooms as of December 31, 2025, with 97% concentrated in the higher-revenue upscale, extended-stay, and midscale segments.
Extended Stay Leadership:
2025 marked the strongest year on record for its extended-stay platform, with 66 domestic extended-stay hotels opened. U.S. extended-stay net rooms grew 11.7% in 2025.
The U.S. extended-stay pipeline reached 30,600 rooms by year-end 2025, with brands like WoodSpring Suites (28 new hotels in 2025) and Everhome Suites (25th opening in 2025) driving this growth.
The company is actively pursuing a conversion strategy for its Suburban Studios brand, aiming to transform 100+ key transient hotels into extended-stay properties, having completed 20 such transformations with 30 more in the pipeline.
Upscale and Core Brand Strengthening:
Initiatives include refreshed brand identities for Comfort and Country Inn & Suites, with a new coffee experience expected across over 2,000 hotels by mid-2026.
The Ascend Collection received a refreshed identity, and a conversion-ready Cambria guestroom was introduced.
The upscale portfolio delivered steady growth in 2025 with 27 U.S. hotels opened across Ascend Collection, Cambria Hotels, and Radisson brands.
Sustainability Mandates:
Choice Hotels published its 2024 Sustainability Report in May 2025, detailing its commitment to integrating sustainable practices.
The company committed to setting science-based targets for reducing greenhouse gas emissions and reported its full Scope 1, 2, and 3 inventory.
A collaboration with the National Park Foundation was announced, including an annual donation of $150,000.
The "Your Community, Your Choice" grant program celebrated its fifth anniversary in 2024, donating $275,000 to local nonprofits.
Portfolio Optimization: Choice Hotels is strategically exiting hotels with lower economic contribution and guest satisfaction to enhance the quality and earnings profile of its system.
3. TECHNOLOGY STRATEGY
Choice Hotels is aggressively moving to an enterprise-wide deployment of artificial intelligence, leveraging its cloud infrastructure and strategic vendor partnerships to improve operations, enhance guest experience, and drive franchisee profitability.
AI Adoption Stance: The company is implementing the next phase of its technology strategy, moving beyond isolated AI pilot projects to production-grade AI deployment at scale across the entire hospitality value chain. This comprehensive integration includes applications in guest discovery and booking, franchise operations, revenue management, maintenance, guest communications, distribution, pricing, and inventory optimization.
Brian Kirkland, Chief Information Officer, stated, "This enterprise-wide adoption of AI is the next chapter in Choice Hotels' history of innovation."
Cloud Migration: Choice Hotels completed the migration of its entire system infrastructure to Amazon Web Services (AWS) cloud in 2024, becoming the first major hotel company to achieve this. This migration provides the foundation for leveraging its data with advanced technologies like large language models and integration with future search engines and social media channels.
Platform Consolidation and Vendor Relationships:

The company also utilizes Salesforce for AgentForce, an AI-powered solution.
Strategic partnerships with Amazon Web Services (AWS) and Salesforce are central to its technology development and deployment.
